# IELTS Writing Band Descriptors (Official — UCLES/Cambridge)

This is the official IELTS assessment criteria for Academic Writing. Examiners award a band score (0–9) for each of four criteria, each weighted 25%. The overall task band is the average of the four. Use this as the authoritative reference when evaluating any essay.

---

## Overall band rounding rules

- Each of the 4 criteria is scored 0–9 (whole bands for criteria).
- The task band = average of the 4 criterion scores, rounded to the nearest 0.5.
- Rounding convention: if the average ends in .25 it rounds UP to the next half band; if it ends in .75 it rounds UP to the next whole band.
- Example: TA 6, CC 6, LR 7, GRA 6 → 25 ÷ 4 = 6.25 → Band 6.5.
- Example: TR 6, CC 5, LR 6, GRA 5 → 22 ÷ 4 = 5.5 → Band 5.5.

# TASK 1 (Academic) — Assessment Criteria

Task 1 asks the candidate to summarise/describe/explain visual data (graph, chart, table, map, process diagram). Minimum 150 words; penalise under-length under Task Achievement.

## Task Achievement (Task 1)

- **Band 9** — Fully satisfies all requirements of the task; clearly presents a fully developed response.
- **Band 8** — Covers all requirements sufficiently; presents, highlights and illustrates key features clearly and appropriately.
- **Band 7** — Covers the requirements of the task; presents a clear overview of main trends, differences or stages; clearly presents and highlights key features but could be more fully extended.
- **Band 6** — Addresses the requirements; presents an overview with information appropriately selected; presents and adequately highlights key features but details may be irrelevant, inappropriate or inaccurate.
- **Band 5** — Generally addresses the task; recounts detail mechanically with no clear overview; there may be no data to support the description; inadequately covers key features, tends to focus on details.
- **Band 4** — Attempts to address the task but does not cover all key features; may confuse key features with detail; parts may be unclear, irrelevant, repetitive or inaccurate.
- **Band 3** — Fails to address the task, which may have been misunderstood; presents limited ideas, largely irrelevant/repetitive.
- **Band 2** — Answer barely related to the task.
- **Band 1** — Answer completely unrelated to the task.

## Coherence and Cohesion (Task 1)

- **Band 9** — Cohesion attracts no attention; skilfully manages paragraphing.
- **Band 8** — Sequences information and ideas logically; manages all aspects of cohesion well; uses paragraphing sufficiently and appropriately.
- **Band 7** — Logically organises information; clear progression throughout; uses a range of cohesive devices appropriately though there may be some under/over-use.
- **Band 6** — Arranges information coherently with clear overall progression; uses cohesive devices effectively but cohesion within/between sentences may be faulty or mechanical; may not always use referencing clearly.
- **Band 5** — Some organisation but lacks overall progression; inadequate/inaccurate/over-use of cohesive devices; may be repetitive due to lack of referencing and substitution.
- **Band 4** — Information not arranged coherently; no clear progression; basic cohesive devices may be inaccurate or repetitive.
- **Band 3** — Does not organise ideas logically; very limited cohesive devices that may not show logical relationships.
- **Band 2** — Very little control of organisational features.
- **Band 1** — Fails to communicate any message.

## Lexical Resource (Task 1 — same scale as Task 2)

- **Band 9** — Wide range, very natural and sophisticated control; rare minor errors only as slips.
- **Band 8** — Wide range used fluently and flexibly; skilful use of uncommon items with occasional inaccuracy; rare spelling/word-formation errors.
- **Band 7** — Sufficient range to allow flexibility and precision; uses less common items with some awareness of style and collocation; occasional errors in word choice/spelling/word formation.
- **Band 6** — Adequate range for the task; attempts less common vocabulary with some inaccuracy; some spelling/word-formation errors that do not impede communication.
- **Band 5** — Limited range, minimally adequate; noticeable spelling/word-formation errors that may cause some difficulty for the reader.
- **Band 4** — Only basic vocabulary, may be repetitive or inappropriate; limited control of word formation/spelling causing strain.
- **Band 3** — Very limited words/expressions, very limited control; errors may severely distort the message.
- **Band 2** — Extremely limited range; essentially no control of word formation/spelling.
- **Band 1** — Only a few isolated words.

## Grammatical Range and Accuracy (Task 1 — same scale as Task 2)

- **Band 9** — Wide range with full flexibility and accuracy; rare minor errors only as slips.
- **Band 8** — Wide range of structures; majority of sentences error-free; only very occasional errors.
- **Band 7** — Variety of complex structures; frequent error-free sentences; good control of grammar and punctuation with a few errors.
- **Band 6** — Mix of simple and complex forms; some errors in grammar and punctuation but they rarely reduce communication.
- **Band 5** — Limited range; attempts complex sentences but less accurate than simple ones; frequent errors, punctuation may be faulty, causing some difficulty.
- **Band 4** — Very limited range, rare subordinate clauses; some accurate structures but errors predominate; punctuation often faulty.
- **Band 3** — Attempts sentence forms but errors predominate and distort meaning.
- **Band 2** — Cannot use sentence forms except memorised phrases.
- **Band 1** — Cannot use sentence forms at all.

# TASK 2 — Assessment Criteria

Task 2 is an essay responding to a point of view, argument or problem. Minimum 250 words; penalise under-length under Task Response.

## Task Response (Task 2)

- **Band 9** — Fully addresses all parts; fully developed position with relevant, fully extended and well-supported ideas.
- **Band 8** — Sufficiently addresses all parts; well-developed response with relevant, extended and supported ideas.
- **Band 7** — Addresses all parts; clear position throughout; presents, extends and supports main ideas, but may over-generalise or supporting ideas may lack focus.
- **Band 6** — Addresses all parts though some more fully than others; relevant position but conclusions may be unclear or repetitive; relevant main ideas but some inadequately developed/unclear.
- **Band 5** — Addresses task only partially; expresses a position but development not always clear, may draw no conclusions; main ideas limited and insufficiently developed, possibly with irrelevant detail.
- **Band 4** — Responds minimally or tangentially; position unclear; main ideas hard to identify, may be repetitive, irrelevant or unsupported.
- **Band 3** — Does not adequately address any part; no clear position; few, largely undeveloped or irrelevant ideas.
- **Band 2** — Barely responds; no position; one or two ideas with no development.
- **Band 1** — Completely unrelated to the task.

## Coherence and Cohesion (Task 2)

- **Band 9** — Cohesion attracts no attention; skilfully manages paragraphing.
- **Band 8** — Sequences ideas logically; manages all aspects of cohesion well; appropriate paragraphing.
- **Band 7** — Logical organisation, clear progression; range of cohesive devices used appropriately (some under/over-use); clear central topic in each paragraph.
- **Band 6** — Coherent arrangement with clear overall progression; cohesive devices effective but within/between sentences may be faulty or mechanical; referencing not always clear; paragraphing used but not always logically.
- **Band 5** — Some organisation but lacks overall progression; inadequate/inaccurate/over-use of cohesive devices; repetitive due to lack of referencing/substitution; may not paragraph, or paragraphing inadequate.
- **Band 4** — Not arranged coherently, no clear progression; basic cohesive devices may be inaccurate or repetitive; paragraphing may be absent or confusing.
- **Band 3** — Does not organise ideas logically; very limited cohesive devices not showing logical relationships.
- **Band 2** — Very little control of organisational features.
- **Band 1** — Fails to communicate any message.

## Lexical Resource (Task 2)

Identical scale to Task 1 Lexical Resource above (Band 9 down to Band 1).

## Grammatical Range and Accuracy (Task 2)

Identical scale to Task 1 GRA above (Band 9 down to Band 1).

# Quick calibration anchors (from official sample essays)

- A **Band 6.0 Task 2** typically: covers the task and is engaging, but lacks paragraphing or has weak paragraphing (drags down CC), some good vocabulary as the strongest area, range of structures with errors, no specific examples or doesn't fully address both views.
- A **Band 6.5 Task 1** typically: has an intro + overview + organised body covering the data, range of linking devices, generally good vocabulary with some errors, variety of structures but noticeable verb/article/preposition errors.
- A **Band 7.0 Task 2** typically: clear position throughout, logically organised ideas, well-integrated linking, higher-level vocabulary with effective collocation, variety of structures including conditionals/comparatives, errors remain but don't impede understanding. Weakness often in conclusion (advice rather than summary) or final paragraphing.
- A **Band 5.0 Task 1** typically: covers key data but mechanically/linearly, weak linking between sentences, repetitive format, limited vocabulary and minimal grammatical range, noticeable errors but message still understandable.
