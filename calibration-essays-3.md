## SAMPLE 17 — Test 1, Task 1 — Official Band 6.0

**Task:** The maps below show an industrial area in the town of Norbiton, and planned future development of the site. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant. Write at least 150 words.

**Visual (two maps — text description):** Compass shown (N/S/E/W). River runs east-west across the north of both maps, with Farmland above (north) and Town to the west (connected by road). A main road runs along the south edge. A roundabout sits in the centre of both maps.

**Now (Norbiton industrial area):**
- Centre: roundabout
- Around roundabout: 6 factory buildings (hatched rectangles), scattered to north, east, and south of the roundabout
- North: river separating farmland from the industrial area
- West: road connecting to Town
- South: main road

**Planned future development:**
- All factories removed and replaced with housing (cross-hatched rectangles)
- Centre: same roundabout, but roads now extend in more directions (north to river, south to main road, and a new southern roundabout added)
- North of river: housing added on the farmland side (new bridge implied by road crossing the river)
- East of roundabout: Playground (top-right), two housing blocks
- South-east of roundabout: School (far right)
- South of roundabout: Medical centre (bottom, near new southern roundabout)
- South-west of roundabout: Shops
- West: housing blocks replacing factories on the left side

**Essay (Band 6.0, text as written):**

> The two maps illustrates the norbiton area in the present days, as well as, the planned development.
>
> Overall, the norbiton industrial area is located at the east side of the town, with a river on the north, separating the farmland from the industrial area, which is located in the center of the map, represented by a few factory biuldings and roads, followed by the main road at the extreme south of the map.
>
> the planned development shows a substantially growth and modifications of the overall infrastructure of the area between the farmland and the main road.
>
> Firstly, the planned development of the norbiton area replaces what once were factories for housings.
>
> Moreover, the roads have been developed to acomodate all the new biuldings that have been planned for the area, which are, a school and a playground to the east side of the roundabout located in the center of the map, as well as shops and a medical center around the round about.
>
> Secondly, a bridge is planned for the north of the map to cross the river and provide acess to the housing that will be located in farmland.

**Per-criterion (inferred from examiner comment):**
- Task Achievement: ~6 — covers key changes (factories to housing, new buildings, bridge); overview present in paragraph 2 but could summarise main changes more clearly; missing detail on housing to the west of the roundabout.
- Coherence & Cohesion: ~6 — ideas arranged coherently; effective cohesive devices ("what once were", "Firstly", "Secondly", "Moreover") and referencing ("which"); sequencing logical.
- Lexical Resource: ~5–6 — some less common vocabulary ("infrastructure", "accommodate"); but spelling frequently inaccurate ("biuldings" / buildings, "acomodate" / accommodate, "acess" / access) and word formation incorrect ("substantially growth" / substantial growth).
- Grammatical Range & Accuracy: ~6 — mix of simple and complex structures; some errors in sentence structure.

**Why 6.0:** CC adequate; TA covers key features but lacks detail; LR dragged down by frequent spelling errors; GRA at 6.

## SAMPLE 18 — Test 1, Task 2 — Official Band 6.5

**Task:** It is important for people to take risks, both in their professional lives and their personal lives. Do you think the advantages of taking risks outweigh the disadvantages? Write at least 250 words.

**Essay (Band 6.5, text as written — NOTE: 242 words, slightly under limit):**

> It is said that taking risks brings a lot of benefits. However, it also gives us some drawbacks.
>
> First of all, it is obvious that taking risks will cause a great loss if people do it and fail. In personal life, this loss might not be so harmful. However, it will be really harmfull in professional life, because people take a responsibility not only for themselves but also others such as colleages, customers and their families. It will even damage the society from the economic point.
>
> On the other hand, we can receive huge benefits by taking risks. Firstly, we can learn how to prepare for one goal through this process. In order to achieve the aim, people will make all the efforts to think about it and try to find more efficient way. If they do this in the professional circumstances, they will recognise the responsibility and importance of cooperation.
>
> Also, it will be completely meaningful even though people can't achieve the goal after taking risks. They will learn the reason why they have failed and how to change it. The failure will enable them to improve their skills and to achieve their object next time.
>
> As I mentioned, it is true that taking risks give us both advantages and disadvantages. However, it can be argued that the benefits outweighed the drawbacks in that we can obtain advantages not only from the result but also from the process of taking risks.

**Per-criterion (inferred from examiner comment):**
- Task Response: ~6 — discusses advantages and disadvantages and gives a clear conclusion (benefits outweigh); puts much greater emphasis on professional life and misses personal life examples; under 250 words (242); no concrete specific examples of actual risks taken.
- Coherence & Cohesion: ~7 — clear progression, ideas logically organised (disadvantages first, then advantages); cohesive devices can be mechanical ("First of all | However | On the other hand | Firstly"); referencing generally appropriate ("it | this loss | They | The failure"); first paragraph very short and paragraphing not entirely logical.
- Lexical Resource: ~6 — effective with some less common items ("damage the society", "receive huge benefits", "enable", "obtain advantages from the process"); occasional errors ("object" / objective, "point" / perspective).
- Grammatical Range & Accuracy: ~7 — good sentence structure, frequent error-free sentences; variety of complex structures including conditionals; a few errors remain.

**Why 6.5:** CC and GRA at ~7; TR and LR at ~6. Word count issue and lack of personal-life examples cap TR. Average ~6.5.

## SAMPLE 19 — Test 2, Task 1 — Official Band 7.5

**Task:** The table and charts below give information on the police budget for 2017 and 2018 in one area of Britain. The table shows where the money came from and the charts show how it was distributed. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant. Write at least 150 words.

**Visual (table + two pie charts — text description):**

**Table — Police Budget 2017–2018 (in £m):**
- National Government: 2017 = £175.5m, 2018 = £177.8m
- Local Taxes: 2017 = £91.2m, 2018 = £102.3m
- Other sources (e.g. grants): 2017 = £38m, 2018 = £38.5m
- Total: 2017 = £304.7m, 2018 = £318.6m

**Two pie charts — "How the money was spent":**
- 2017: Salaries (officers and staff) = 75%, Buildings and transport = 17%, Technology = 8%
- 2018: Salaries (officers and staff) = 69%, Technology = 14%, Buildings and transport = 17%

Key changes: total budget increased from £304.7m to £318.6m; Local Taxes saw the biggest proportional rise; salary share fell from 75% to 69%; technology nearly doubled from 8% to 14%; buildings and transport stayed constant at 17%.

**Essay (Band 7.5, text as written):**

> The table illustrates the data on the police budget in which the money came from and the two pie charts describe the distribution of the amount of money in the two of year 2017 and 2018 in an area of Britain. Overall, there was an upward trend in all three different sources while the money spent on salaries was always the majority of contribution.
>
> Looking into more details, the highest amount of money on the police budget belonged to 'National Government', 175.5 million pounds in 2017 and it kept rising to 177.8 million pounds. Thus was followed by 'Local Taxes', at 91.2 million pounds in 2017, after one year, it increase significantly to 102.3 million pounds.
>
> In term of the how the money was spent, the majority of police budget goes to salaries which was for officers and staff, dropping slightly from 75% in 2017 to 69% in 2018. Meanwhile, the proportion of 'Buildings and transport' remained constantly, at 17% each year. An opposite pattern can be seen in the category of technology, its figure rose sharply from 8% in 2017 to 14% in 2018, which was always the lowest rate during the given period.

**Per-criterion (inferred from examiner comment):**
- Task Achievement: ~7 — clear overview at end of first paragraph; full details for first two sources; key features of pie charts covered; key features from the table could be more fully extended (other sources and total figures barely mentioned).
- Coherence & Cohesion: ~8 — information in logical order (table first, then charts); three paragraphs appropriately structured; good range of cohesive devices ("while", "which was"); well managed.
- Lexical Resource: ~8 — wide range with accurate spelling ("distribution", "majority of contribution", "figure rose sharply", "during the given period"); occasional errors in word choice.
- Grammatical Range & Accuracy: ~7 — wide range including modal and continuous forms ("kept rising", "can be seen"); occasional errors (present tense "goes" for past data, "it increase" / increased, "In term" / In terms).

**Why 7.5:** Strong CC and LR; TA and GRA at 7 due to missing table details and occasional errors. Average rounds to 7.5.

## SAMPLE 20 — Test 2, Task 2 — Official Band 6.5

**Task:** Some children spend hours every day on their smartphones. Why is this the case? Do you think this is a positive or a negative development? Write at least 250 words.

**Essay (Band 6.5, text as written):**

> Mobile phones, nowadays, contains essential features with entertainment also. There has been a large growth seen in usage hours of smartphones among youngsters. There are several reasons behind this situation and I find this development more beneficial than negative. Both the reasons and my view is elaborated further.
>
> The first reason for overusage of smart devices by youngsters is the social benefit they provide. The smart phone connected with internet opens up the large possibilities, from creating new friends to communicating with them over social media. For instance, a child in my neighbourhood chats for hours with his school friends over Facebook (a social media) and also spend time over online video sharing phone application. Moreover, the mobile gaming, specially multiplayer games, is another major reason for the situation. Children plays different kind of games over mobile for the entertainment purpose and they involve themselves in games in such a manner, that they forget about the timing and other work to do.
>
> However, I believe that smartphones have also increased the knowledge of pupils. It has developed some important social skills, such as communication skill, team work and many more, by allowing them to work and play in groups, without the restriction of distance. In addition, children can learn through internet by watchin online videos and reading articles, which ultimately helps them in their studies as well as language skills. For example, whenever my niece require to know about something, he searches it over the internet and learns from it. Moreover, multiplayer online gaming improves their multitasking ability and it also gives them a competitive environment
>

**Per-criterion (inferred from examiner comment):**
- Task Response: ~7 — addresses both parts (reasons + positive/negative); position stated in opening paragraph; reasons given (social benefit, gaming) with relevant examples; benefits elaborated in paragraph 3; clear conclusion.
- Coherence & Cohesion: ~7 — logically organised across four paragraphs with clear progression; range of cohesive devices ("The first reason", "For instance", "Moreover", "However", "In addition"); referencing appropriate ("they", "themselves", "their studies", "it").
- Lexical Resource: ~6 — good range with higher-level items ("social skills", "restriction of distance", "ultimately"); few errors ("overusage" / overuse, "niece...he" / she, "watchin" / watching, "require to know" / needs to know).

**Why 6.5:** TR and CC at ~7; LR and GRA at ~6. Average = 6.5.

## SAMPLE 21 — Test 3, Task 1 — Official Band 5.5

**Task:** The chart below gives information about how families in one country spent their weekly income in 1968 and in 2018. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant. Write at least 150 words.

**Visual (horizontal bar chart — text description):** Title "1968 and 2018: average weekly spending by families". X-axis = % of weekly income (0–40%). Two bars per category (black = 1968, grey = 2018). Eight categories:
- **Food:** 1968 ~35%, 2018 ~17% — biggest drop
- **Housing:** 1968 ~10%, 2018 ~19% — significant rise
- **Fuel and power:** 1968 ~6%, 2018 ~4% — slight drop
- **Clothing and footwear:** 1968 ~10%, 2018 ~5% — halved

- **Leisure:** 1968 ~9%, 2018 ~23% — biggest rise

Key overview: in 1968 food dominated; by 2018 leisure is the biggest category. Housing and transport also rose significantly; food, clothing, and personal goods fell.

**Essay (Band 5.5, text as written):**

> The chart describes the data about families weekly expenditure prospects in 1968 and in 2018.
>
> The most significant spent rate is on food with a 35% ratio in the year 1968. Housing and clothing come next with the same prospect of 10% of the weekly income. Expenditure on leisure, transport, personal goods and household goods are almost same percentage, the former one is slightly more. Lastly, spent rates on fuel and the others are recorded the least with a 6% in the year of 1968.

It had soared abut 10% in 50 years. Transportation expeditures comes after and followed by Household goods and the othe categories respetively. Last three had remained the least just as in 1968 which is fuel and power, clothing and footwear and personal goods.

**Per-criterion (inferred from examiner comment):**

- Coherence & Cohesion: ~6 — four paragraphs, clear overall progression; cohesive devices used ("Turning to 2018", "followed by", "whereas") with some referencing ("former one", "the othe categories", "Last three").
- Lexical Resource: ~5–6 — some less common examples ("dropped dramatically to", "rised significantly", "soared"); errors ("spent rates" / expenditure, "housing spent" / housing costs) do not impede communication.

Therefore, they should be allowed to improve their lives outside the limitations of their country of origin. For example, whilst in Spain residents in a hospital do not have hands-on experience due to safety measures, in other countries such as Argentina, residents actually practice their skills with patients. ## SAMPLE 23 — Test 4, Task 1 — Official Band 7.5

**Task:** The graph below shows the number of shops that closed and the number of new shops that opened in one country between 2011 and 2018. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant. Write at least 150 words.

**Visual (line graph — text description):** Title "Number of shop closures and openings 2011–2018". Y-axis = number of shops (0–9,000). X-axis = years 2011–2018. Two lines:
- **Closures** (solid): 2011 ~6,400; 2012 ~6,000; 2013 ~7,000; 2014 ~6,500; 2015 ~700 (dramatic fall, lowest point); 2016 ~5,200; 2017 ~5,000; 2018 ~5,100.
- **Openings** (dashed): 2011 ~8,500 (highest point); 2012 ~4,000; 2013 ~5,000; 2014 ~6,000; 2015 ~4,000; 2016 ~4,000; 2017 ~4,200; 2018 ~3,000 (lowest point).

Key features: openings started much higher than closures in 2011 but fell sharply; closures fluctuated 2011–2014 then had a dramatic fall in 2015 before rebounding; by 2018 closures (~5,100) exceeded openings (~3,000); openings showed an overall downward trend.

**Essay (Band 7.5, text as written):**

> The line graph shows trends in shop closures and openings of new shops in a particular country between the years 2011 and 2018.
>
> In 2011 approximately 6,400 shops closed. The number of closures fluctuated over the next four years until 2015, when there was a dramatic fall in closures to roughly 700 shops. The following year the number of shops closing their doors rose sharply, reaching over 5,000. The figures remained steady for the next two years, with just over 5,000 closures in 2018.
>
> The number of new shops opening decreased dramatically between 2011 (approximately 8,500) and 2012 (just under 4,000) but rebounded by roughly 50% by 2014. In 2015, the number of openings then fell to the 2012 level, but remained stable for the next two years. The last recorded year, 2018, saw a further fall to 3,000 new openings, the lowest point in this seven year period.
>
> Overall, the number of shop closures has remained within the 5,000 to 7,000 range (with the exception of 2015). In contrast, new shop openings have shown a wider range of figures, but generally indicate a downward trend over the same period.

**Per-criterion (inferred from examiner comment):**
- Task Achievement: ~7–8 — clear overview in final paragraph; key features highlighted appropriately (dramatic fall in closures 2015, dramatic drop in openings 2011-2012); closures and openings dealt with separately with clear detail; peaks and low points flagged; could provide more detail during the fluctuation periods (2011–2014 and 2016–2018).
- Coherence & Cohesion: ~8 — logically organised (closures paragraph, openings paragraph, overview); paragraphing appropriate (apart from single-sentence intro); cohesion well managed.
- Lexical Resource: ~8 — wide range with skilful use ("rebounded by roughly 50%", "further fall", "exception of", "dramatic fall"); accurate spelling.
- Grammatical Range & Accuracy: ~7 — variety of complex structures with numerous long multi-clause sentences; mostly accurate.

**Why 7.5:** Strong across all criteria; TA and GRA at ~7, CC and LR at ~8. Average = 7.5.

## SAMPLE 24 — Test 4, Task 2 — Official Band 6.0

**Task:** Nowadays, a growing number of people with health problems are trying alternative medicines and treatments instead of visiting their usual doctor. Do you think this is a positive or a negative development? Write at least 250 words.

**Essay (Band 6.0, text as written):**

Nowadays, more and more people with some diseases decide to use alternative medicines instead of classic medicine. In this essay I will try to discuss pros and cons.
>
> In my opinion, the disadvantages outweigh the advantages of using traditional medicine. The first reason is that nobody knows how this treatment will affect to a person's health. There are a lot of cases when using different herbals caused allergic reaction and some people dead. The next reason is that people who do not have any medical education try alternative medicines. They do not know what the result will be and hope that it will be positive but not always is like that.
>

The first and the main pro is that using herbals does not cause environmental problems such as air pollution or gas waste. Many pharmaceutical plants use chemicals which have harmful affect on the environment. The other reason is that alternative medicines are usually much cheaper than usual treatment as you do not have to go to pharmacy and buy expensive drugs.
>

**Per-criterion (inferred from examiner comment):**
