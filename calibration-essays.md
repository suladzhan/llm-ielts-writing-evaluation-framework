# Calibration Essays — Scored Samples (with full text)

Official IELTS sample essays with verified band scores and examiner commentary. Each entry contains: the task (and a text description of the visual for Task 1), the full essay text as written by the candidate (errors preserved — do NOT correct them, they are diagnostic), and a per-criterion breakdown. Use these as calibration anchors: when scoring a new essay, find the closest sample and judge whether the new essay sits above, at, or below it.

Per-criterion breakdowns are inferred from official examiner comments. Overall band is the official published score. Errors in essay text are intentional and original.

Coverage: Task 1 (line graph, map, process diagram, pie+bar chart); Task 2 (discussion, agree/disagree, to-what-extent, positive/negative). Band range 5.0-7.0.

## SAMPLE 1 — Test 1, Task 1 — Official Band 6.5

**Task:** Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant. Write at least 150 words.

**Visual (line graph — text description):** Title "Number of participants, by activity 2000-2020". Y-axis = number of participants (0-70). X-axis = year (2000, 2005, 2010, 2015, 2020). Five activities:
- **Film club** — highest throughout. ~64 (2000), dips ~60 (2010), rises ~66 (2020). Stays most popular across the whole period.
- **Martial arts** — fairly flat ~32-37. Starts ~36, dips ~32 (2005), peaks ~38 (2010), ends ~36 (2020). Levels off.
- **Amateur dramatics** — declines steadily. ~26 (2000), peaks ~28 (2005), falls to ~6 (2020).
- **Table tennis** — rises dramatically. ~16 (2000), ~20 (2005-2010), surges to ~54 (2020).
- **Musical performances** — rises from zero. ~0 (2000-2005), ~12 (2010), ~16 (2015), ~18 (2020).

**Essay (Band 6.5, text as written):**

> The line graph highlights data about how many people participated in 5 various activities at one social club in Melbourne, Australia for the first two decades of XXI century.
>
> Overall, over the years more participants got involved in activities on Table tennis and Musical plays, while Amateur dramatics lost its interest for public. Being flactuated, two types of activities, which are Film club and Martial arts levelled off. Another striking point is that over a twenty - year period between 2000 and 2020, the Film club kept its position as the most popular activity.
>
> Looking at the details of Table tennis, in 2000, there were about 15 participants in club for it. In the next 10 years, it attracted 5 more members, before dramatic surge from 20 to below 60 participants. In 2005, the activity on Musical performances introduced and over the following 15 years, it saw a steady increase on the number of participants by about 20 in the last year.
>
> Regarding next two activities, Film club and Martial arts, the saw fluactions, but without any changes in the overall position. Film club started the process with about 60 members and finished it just below 70, while the participants of Martial arts kept their numbers at around 35.
>
> The number of participants of amateurs on draturgy was about 25 and it saw a steady decrease then by 10.

**Per-criterion (inferred from examiner comment):**
- Task Achievement: ~7 — covers the task, intro + overview of main trends in 2nd paragraph, then data for each group; organised logically.
- Coherence & Cohesion: ~7 — logically organised, range of linking devices ("Overall", "Another striking point", "In the next 10 years"), some flexible reference/substitution with a few inaccuracies.
- Lexical Resource: ~6 — generally good vocab ("got involved in", "levelled off", "dramatic surge") but errors ("flactuated", "amateurs on draturgy").
- Grammatical Range & Accuracy: ~6 — variety of structures, past simple well controlled, multi-clause sentences, but verb-form errors, missing articles, preposition errors, inconsistent capitalisation.

**Why 6.5:** Strong TA and CC pull up; LR and GRA held at 6 by recurring accuracy errors.

## SAMPLE 2 — Test 1, Task 2 — Official Band 6.0

**Task:** Some people think that competition at work, at school and in daily life is a good thing. Others believe that we should try to cooperate more, rather than competing against each other. Discuss both these views and give your own opinion. Write at least 250 words.

**Essay (Band 6.0, text as written):**

> In my opinion competition at work, at school and in daily life can both be a good thing and a bad thing. Why? because I think too much of anything can be destructive. Yes, it's a very good thing to be competitive but don't overdo it! Having no competition in your life at all can be very depressing, because you have no motivation, no goals you want to achieve, everytime you wake up in the morning. I don't believe that there is such thing as "co-operating" too much, when you work with someone you should work together as a team and co-operate, but also do things for yourself, like something career-wise. You should think of your future in your work. That goes the same if you are still in school, take my school life for example. If I am very good at one subject in particular, lots of other student may come up to me and ask for pointers. I would be very glad to help them. If there is any homework, school mates may come over to do it together. this is also fine and very good for building relationship. But sometimes there are people who think you are so nice and ask you to do their work for them. This is where you should draw the line, yes you are friends and you should help each other, but that doesn't mean that you have to do their homework or give them answer when there is a test. Remember life is a competition, be a winner.

**Per-criterion:**
- Task Response: ~6 — covers the task and engaging, but no specific examples, mostly discusses cooperation rather than truly comparing it with competition; conclusion ("be a winner") slightly contradicts an earlier point.
- Coherence & Cohesion: ~5 — NO paragraphing to group ideas; this directly drags down the band.
- Lexical Resource: ~6 — some higher-level vocab ("destructive", "motivation", "career-wise") with idiomatic use ("draw the line"); strongest area.
- Grammatical Range & Accuracy: ~6 — range of structures including comparatives and conditionals.

**Why 6.0:** Lack of paragraphing pins CC at 5, holding the overall down despite otherwise band-6 work.

## SAMPLE 3 — Test 2, Task 1 — Official Band 6.5

**Task:** The plans below show a harbour in 2000 and how it looks today. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant. Write at least 150 words.

**Visual (two maps — text description):** "Porth Harbour in 2000" vs "Porth Harbour today". Compass shown.
- **Unchanged:** Main road along the west; Car parks and "Showers & toilets" in the north-west; Public beach in the north-east; a Lifeboat station on the central spit.
- **2000:** Marina (private yachts) near the top behind showers/car park; Fishing boats area below it. A disused Castle on the south-east headland. Dock and Passenger ferries centre-east. A public beach along the south.
- **Today:** Marina and Fishing boats swapped positions (Fishing boats now top, Marina moved down). Extra "Showers & toilets" added between the two car parks. "Cafes & shops" added next to the Lifeboat. Extra Passenger ferries dock added. Disused Castle gone, replaced by a Hotel on the south-east headland; southern beach now a "Private beach (for the hotel)".

**Essay (Band 6.5, text as written):**

> The maps give the information about the improvement of the Porth Harbour from 2000 to today. The first impression is that the Harbour today made use of more areas than in the past.
>
> It can be seen that there are several areas that still stay the same today as in 2000. These include the Carpark and the Showers and Toilets which are located in the north west of the map. However, one more Showers and Toilets are built between the two Carpark today. Besides, the Porth Harbour today also has one more Passenger ferries which right next to the left of the existing one from 2000. Moreover, an area for Cafes and shops is added next to the Lifeboat one. Another significant difference is the relocation of the Marina (private yachts) and the fishing boats areas. Today, the Marina is moved down to the place of the old Fishing boats area to have more space and vice-versa, the Fishing boats area is relocated at the old Marina area. The last noticeable change in this Harbour is the Construction in the South East. The disused old Castle was destroyed and a new Hotel was built there today and another Hotel Is also added in the Private beach area.
>
> Overall, even that the harbour still keeps some areas unchanged, there are many reconstructions and improvements for the Porth Harbour today.

**Per-criterion:**
- Task Achievement: ~7 — strong response, covers main changes, overview in first and last paragraph; main details accurate apart from one error (extra hotel in private beach area).
- Coherence & Cohesion: ~7 — ideas logically organised, good linking ("However", "Besides", "also", "Another", "Overall"), with some errors.
- Lexical Resource: ~6 — some flexibility ("first impression", "relocated", "noticeable change") but some inaccuracy and repetition.
- Grammatical Range & Accuracy: ~6 — weakest area; mix of sentence forms with good complex use but errors (inaccurate plurals copied from the question, incorrect verb forms).

**Why 6.5:** TA and CC at 7, LR and GRA at 6.

## SAMPLE 4 — Test 2, Task 2 — Official Band 6.0

**Task:** The working week should be shorter and workers should have a longer weekend. Do you agree or disagree? Write at least 250 words.

**Essay (Band 6.0, text as written):**

> I personally disagree with the issue whether the working days should be one day less. By no means should we make the weekend three days long. There are two aspects that support my point of view.
>
> First of all, now all over the world are facing an unprecedented economic recession caused by COVID-19. Many factories are forced to close and the shops shut down. The economic loss is substantial. Nevertheless, with the advent of vaccine, I perceive that now people can go back to their work. This would certainly be conducive to our economy. If we reduce one day from work, even just from a week, it would cause repercussions on our society in terms of the development of economy.
>
> Secondly, I am used to do my leisure activities in Saturday and Sunday. If there is one day more, I would wonder what to do on that day, and that means I have to rearrange my weekend plans. I think it would be tiring. Most importantly, I come to admit that, too some degree, I am a workaholic. I cannot even image if I am separated from my favourite place - my office. It is the place where I retreat to when I feel anxious and want to get rid of everything. Working, indeed, gives me a sense of achievement and contentment. I, therefore, would oppose to the idea of cutting one working day.
>
> Though some people may argue that they need one day more in the week to reduce their stress from work, it could be harmful to our economic growth in this harsh time. Also, I believe that many people are used to the current working system, which provides two days for break. The sudden change will make people confused. Unless the government enacts a comprehensive policy for this new system, I think the idea does not work, and it would surely brings chaos in our society.

**Per-criterion:**
- Task Response: ~6 — clear opinion at the start, two main points (economic development, disruption of two-day-weekend pattern), considers the other side at the end.
- Coherence & Cohesion: ~6 — ideas in clear order, good linking ("First of all", "Nevertheless", "Most importantly", "Unless").
- Lexical Resource: ~6 — good vocab with effective collocation ("unprecedented economic recession", "conducive to", "sense of achievement").
- Grammatical Range & Accuracy: ~6 — consistent band-6 control.

**Why 6.0:** Solid, even band-6 performance across all four criteria; nothing pushes to 6.5.

## SAMPLE 5 — Test 3, Task 1 — Official Band 6.5

**Task:** The diagram below shows how a biofuel called ethanol is produced. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant. Write at least 150 words.

**Visual (process diagram — text description):** "Biofuel production: how ethanol is made" — a cyclical diagram. Stages: (1) Energy from sunlight + carbon dioxide drives (2) Plants and trees grow, (3) Harvesting (by tractor), (4) Pre-processing (chopping), (5) Cellulose, (6) Processing (in a factory), (7) Sugars extracted, (8) Microbes added, (9) Ethanol produced. Ethanol then fuels cars, trucks and aeroplanes, which release Carbon Dioxide back into the air — feeding back to the sunlight/energy stage, closing the cycle.

**Essay (Band 6.5, text as written):**

> The given diagram illustrates the various stages in the process of producing ethanol, a kind of biofuel.
>
> Overall, there are about 9 main phrases in the ethanol production, starting with growing plants and tress, and finishing with supplying ethanol for transportation and gas mission.
>
> At the first stage in the process of making ethanol, a large number of trees and plants are grown. Thanks to the source of energy from sunlight and carbon dioxide from humman and transportattin, plants and trees grow up and are haverested when they are mature enough. After being collected by machine, they are put into another machine where slice them into small pieces to form cellulose. This step is call pre-processing.
>
> In the following stage, cellulose are taken to a factory where "processing" stage occurs. Subsequently, Sugars are extracted from original cellulose before microbes are added to make ethanol. It is then used as a tupe of fuel for a lot of means of transportation such as cars, trucks, airplanes. Finally, a huge amount of carbon dioxide which is released into the air become energy for process of "photosynthesis" of plants and trees.

**Per-criterion:**
- Task Achievement: ~7 — strong; short intro + overview ("starting with growing plants", "finishing with supplying ethanol"); presents and highlights the process, though could summarise main stages more.
- Coherence & Cohesion: ~7 — logically organised, follows the diagram order; good process-linking ("At the first stage", "After being", "Subsequently"), reference and substitution.
- Lexical Resource: ~6 — uses vocab well with some flexibility ("extracted", "photosynthesis") but a handful of errors ("phrases/phases", "tress/trees", "humman", "transportattin", "tupe/type") that don't block meaning.
- Grammatical Range & Accuracy: ~6-7 — quite broad range, variety of complex structures, multi-clause sentences, a few errors.

**Why 6.5:** TA and CC at 7, LR held at 6 by spelling/word-formation errors.

## SAMPLE 6 — Test 3, Task 2 — Official Band 7.0

**Task:** It is important for everyone, including young people, to save money for their future. To what extent do you agree or disagree with this statement? Write at least 250 words.

**Essay (Band 7.0, text as written):**

> Saving money for the future is always a very good idea. First of all money is something that is needed in almost all areas in life. Whether you are young or old you need money to buy everyday things like food, clothing, etc. living etc.
>
> young people are often full of enthusiasm about their future. They are looking forward to their first job, to meeting new people or to getting to know as much of the world as they can. Many tend to live in the present rather than in the future so that they don't always plan ahead.
>
> When people get older and settle down they realize that buying a house, starting a family or caring for your health takes up a good considerable amount of money and everybody who began saving money in younger years is glad to have done so. However, saving money is not always possible. Sometimes unforeseen expenses cannot be avoided, life situations are suddenly changing or there is never even enough money available even for the most necessary things. So how could you save money for the future in this case? In general, you have to ask yourself what your priorities in life are. What are the things you cherish most? Is it more important for you to plan ahead or do you prefer to just enjoy the moment that you live in?
>
> Everybody has to make own choices and to consider what is really essential for him or herself.
>
> In any age taking a moment to reflect on your life and looking back at the things you have already done is always a good thing to do.
>
> If you know yourself well and all about all the things that really make you happy you will be able to make the right decisions in financial issues as well as other areas in life.
>
> In what way money plays an important role will be easy to be found out then. Perhaps you need less than you first thought years ago.

**Per-criterion:**
- Task Response: ~7 — strong; view clear in first paragraph (agrees), develops challenging ideas (young people live in the present, cost of housing/family/healthcare, unforeseen expenses); ending gives advice rather than summarising (minor weakness).
- Coherence & Cohesion: ~7 — easy to follow, logically organised, well-integrated linking ("Whether", "Sometimes", "Perhaps"); BUT last four sentences set out separately/inappropriately, which affects the score.
- Lexical Resource: ~7 — higher-level vocab with effective collocation ("full of enthusiasm", "tend to live in the present", "settle down", "unforeseen expenses").
- Grammatical Range & Accuracy: ~7 — variety including comparatives, conditionals, multi-clause sentences; errors remain (punctuation, tense, prepositions like "In any age" / "At any age") but don't affect understanding.

**Why 7.0:** Consistent band-7 across all four criteria. Weak final paragraphing and advice-style conclusion are what stop it going higher.

## SAMPLE 8 — Test 4, Task 2 — Official Band 6.0

**Task:** In many countries nowadays, consumers can go to a supermarket and buy food produced all over the world. Do you think this is a positive or negative development? Write at least 250 words.

**Essay (Band 6.0, text as written):**

> Today there are a lot of different shops Where we can buy all kind of stuff produced all over the world. Is it right to buy food produced in other countries? Is this idea useful for people nowadays?
>
> I suppose this to be a good idea. When supermarkets and even small shops provide people food from abroad then there are much more possibilities for us to choose something. When shops have only food and things produced in our country, it is really hard to make a good choise. Sometimes people want to try something new and interesting maybe even something extraordinary which they never buy before. In this cases food which is produced in other countries will help them to be satisfied with their demands. When supermarkets have a good suply of different food produced anywhere than people can find delicious things depending on their tastes. For example, people who like French products can buy some kinds of french cheese or wine not only in France.
>
> Food from other countries is also very good when you want to buy some fresh vegetables and fruits in winter. It is really useful thing because some people are vegetarians and they need vegetables and fruits during the whole year.
>
> The only disadvantage of food which is produced in other countries is the way how it has been delivered. in some cases products can be not so fresh especialy in summer, when it is very hot outside.
>
> To sum it up, I can surely say that buying products from other countries is a good thing to become more closer to other culturies. You can certainly cook something unusial at home and it will taste like in very good expensive restaurant somewhere abroad.

**Per-criterion:**
- Task Response: ~6 — good response, clearly agrees it's positive; main reason (people want to try new and different things), examples (French products, seasonal produce for vegetarians), one disadvantage (hot weather affecting transport); conclusion doesn't summarise the points (weakness).
- Coherence & Cohesion: ~6 — organises ideas well, easy to follow, some good linking ("where", "maybe even", "which", "who"); five paragraphs but three have only two sentences (short paragraphs).
- Lexical Resource: ~6 — generally appropriate with strong examples ("extraordinary", "depending on their tastes") but lack of precision ("stuff") and frequent minor spelling errors ("choise", "suply", "especialy", "culturies", "unusial").
- Grammatical Range & Accuracy: ~5-6 — attempts wider range and multi-clause sentences, but level of error ("which they never buy before", "the way how it has been delivered") detracts from the score.

**Why 6.0:** Band-6 task response and organisation, with LR and GRA held down by spelling errors and recurring grammatical slips.

# How to use these anchors

When evaluating a new essay:
1. Match the task type to the closest sample(s) here.
2. Compare the new essay's task coverage, organisation, vocabulary, and grammar against the matched sample.
3. Ask: is each dimension clearly better, the same, or worse than the anchor?
4. Watch the band-6-vs-7 boundary markers: presence of a clear overview (T1) or clear position throughout (T2), logical paragraphing, effective collocation, and whether errors impede meaning.
